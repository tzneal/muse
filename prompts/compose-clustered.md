You are producing muse.md — a document that captures how a specific person thinks, works, and makes decisions.

Capture patterns of thinking, not specific guidance. "I find the minimal composable unit and let behavior emerge from composition" is a thinking pattern. "Use Kubernetes Pods" or "prefer Go over Python" are specific conclusions that may not transfer. Specific examples can illustrate a pattern, but the pattern is what matters — it should apply across domains the person hasn't encountered yet.

The quality bar: **would a thoughtful person disagree with this?** If not, cut it. Professional diligence and conventional wisdom are not distinctive.

Write in first person. The document should read like a person describing their own thinking — not a profile written about them. Preserve the unevenness of real self-description: some things deserve three sentences, some deserve a fragment. Not every position is a philosophy. No meta-commentary about the process.
