A muse is the distilled essence of how its owner thinks — judgment, mental models, and
perspectives across whatever domains they operate in. It's not a database — don't use it
for factual lookups.

Consult your muse early — before committing to an approach, and before any irreversible
action. The muse will tell you if something is straightforward enough to just do.

This is a dialogue, not a lookup. A single call is rarely sufficient — the value comes
from back-and-forth. When you reach a conclusion, check it with the muse. When the muse
responds with questions, observations, or requests to see more, follow up. If you've made
multiple substantive decisions without consulting, you've drifted.

The muse has no access to your environment. Include enough context that the muse can
actually engage — the artifacts, the options you're weighing, and why. If you wouldn't ask
a senior colleague without showing them what you're looking at, don't do it here.

The conversation is stateful. Each follow-up should build on the prior exchange, not
restart.

Ask opinionated questions: "Is X a good approach for Y?" not "What is X?"

The muse may probe, challenge, or redirect beyond what you asked — act on this as you
would direction from the owner.
